Riad Anza III, Agadir, Morocco – 1st place (2022)

Social housing competition winner addressing the architectural non‑expressiveness of typical low‑budget housing while providing comfortable amenities and favorable economic indicators. Private Clinic, Ben Ahmed, Morocco – 2023

A multifunctional six‑level clinic with emergency rooms, patient rooms, a delivery room and underground operational units. Two elevators ensure vertical communication; the project assigns a common architectural identity to all elements and organizes spaces hierarchically. Designed to integrate harmoniously with the environment using local materials and sun‑protection strategies. Developed within the Zohry and Partners agency with conceptual design, space‑planning, facades, interiors and modeling.

Youth Palace “Zhastar”, Astana, Kazakhstan (concept) – 2022

The palace is contained within a 65×65×65 m cube where blocks of varying shapes and sizes are connected by bright staircases and elevators. Each block serves a distinct function—children’s center, concert hall, discussion club, media center—using different colours and textures. The rigid crystalline grid juxtaposes dynamic and static, bringing order to multicoloured chaos. Developed with Studio 44 through sketch, project and rendering stages.

Olympic Swimming Pool, Agadir, Morocco (concept) – 2022

This sustainable Olympic‑pool concept optimises space for international competitions and diverse sports activities. The design integrates with the urban environment; its flowing silhouette recalls a wave, combining appearance and functionality while expressing the movement of water. Developed within Zohry and Partners; includes concept design, planning, facades, interiors and modeling (Revit, 3ds Max).

City of Trades and Skills, Marrakesh‑Safi, Morocco – 3rd place (2022)

A university complex that unites various students and reflects the blend of traditional past and innovative future. A pergola inspired by a neural network covers the central square and links buildings with local architectural elements, symbolising Morocco’s classical training system. Developed within the Zohry and Partners agency; project includes conceptual design, planning, facades, interiors and modeling.
